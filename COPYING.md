## 4. ETHICAL BOUNDARIES (SCOPE)

The licenses in §§1–2 are granted only for uses that are not Prohibited Uses and only to Entities that are not Prohibited Entities.

### 4.1 Prohibited Uses (non-exhaustive):

(a) Development, operation, or maintenance of fossil-fuel extraction, production, or refining (coal, crude oil, tar/bitumen, natural gas). Personal consumer use of energy/fuel is not prohibited.

(b) Design, deployment, or operation of systems intended for war crimes, crimes against humanity, or violations of Common Article 3 of the Geneva Conventions; targeted mass repression (e.g., mass surveillance to persecute protected groups); or incitement to genocide.

(c) Weapons of mass destruction (nuclear, biological, chemical) or delivery systems primarily designed for such.

(d) Deliberate large-scale environmental destruction, including activities reasonably expected to accelerate climate catastrophe (e.g., new thermal-coal developments) or accelerate eco-spheric destruction.

(e) Training, tuning, or operating models to generate, facilitate, or direct any of the above.

### 4.2 Prohibited Entities:

(a) DHRs and their Agents.

(b) **Fossil-Fuel Industry Exclusion:** Entities directly involved in the extraction, production, or refining of fossil fuels as a primary business (≥50% of Consolidated Revenue). This exclusion does not apply to individual consumers purchasing fossil-fuel energy for personal use.

(b.1) **Eco-Sphere Destruction Exclusion:** Entities whose primary business (≥50% of Consolidated Revenue) is derived from large-scale ecosystem destruction, including but not limited to deforestation of primary forests, large-scale ocean habitat destruction, or systematic biodiversity eradication. This exclusion does not apply to subsistence-level activities by vulnerable or Indigenous communities.

(c) **War Crimes:** Entities credibly determined, within the past five (5) years, to have engaged in war crimes, crimes against humanity, or grave breaches of the Geneva Conventions. A "credible determination" under this subsection includes, without limitation:

(i) formal charge or finding of guilt by an internationally recognized court or tribunal;

(ii) public statement or report by OHCHR or comparable United Nations body;

(iii) Credible Reporting establishing a Documented Pattern of Conduct; or

(iv) other credible public evidence sufficient to support the Contributor's Good-Faith Determination.

(d) **Discrimination, Harassment, or Violence Against Protected or Vulnerable Groups:** Entities credibly determined, within the past five (5) years, to have engaged in or systemically tolerated discrimination, harassment, intimidation, or violence directed at women, LGBTQ+ persons, racial or ethnic minorities, religious minorities, Indigenous peoples, persons with disabilities, or any other Protected or Vulnerable Group. "Credible determination" has the meaning given in subsection (c).

(e) **Human-Rights Violators:** Entities credibly determined, within the past five (5) years, to have engaged in systemic human-rights violations. "Credible determination" has the meaning given in subsection (c).

(f) **Officer Misconduct and Failure to Remediate:** An Entity becomes a Prohibited Entity if a member of its executive leadership, board of directors, or controlling ownership engages in a Documented Pattern of Conduct constituting harassment, abuse, intimidation, sexual misconduct, or incitement of harassment or violence against women or members of any Protected or Vulnerable Group, and the Entity fails within one hundred eighty (180) days from the conduct becoming publicly known to:

(i) remove the individual from their position;

(ii) divest the individual's controlling interest;

(iii) publicly disavow the conduct and implement enforceable structural reform; or

(iv) take comparable measures meaningfully ending the individual's authority and capacity to repeat the conduct within or through the Entity.

This subsection captures the toleration of harm by institutional inaction. An Entity that promptly remediates remains licensed; an Entity that retains the individual past the 180-day window demonstrates institutional choice and is treated as having adopted the conduct as its own.

(g) **Platform Amplification of Targeted Harassment:** An Entity becomes a Prohibited Entity if it owns, operates, or controls a Platform that is used by its leadership, ownership, employees acting in concert with leadership, or with the Entity's acquiescence — including through algorithmic amplification, selective enforcement of stated content policies, or willful failure to enforce stated content policies — to direct, incite, organize, or substantially amplify harassment campaigns against named individuals on the basis of:

(i) their membership in a Protected or Vulnerable Group;

(ii) their journalism or public-interest investigation;

(iii) their political speech, dissent, or advocacy; or

(iv) their professional, scholarly, or artistic work on matters of public concern.

For purposes of this subsection, "acquiescence" includes the deliberate construction or maintenance of platform features, recommendation systems, or moderation practices that predictably amplify such harassment, where the Entity has been made aware of the pattern and has not taken reasonable steps to disrupt it.

### 4.2.1 Clock-Reset Rule

For purposes of §4.2(c)–(g), the five-year period is measured from the date of the most recent Qualifying Event applicable to the Entity, calculated as of the date the Entity exercises or seeks to exercise rights under this License, and re-evaluated as of the date a Contributor invokes §6.

**Each new Qualifying Event independently restarts the five-year period.** Multiple Qualifying Events have cumulative, not concurrent, effect. An Entity that is the subject of repeated Qualifying Events therefore remains a Prohibited Entity continuously, without interruption, for so long as new events occur within five years of each prior event.

For §4.2(f) and §4.2(g), the Qualifying Event is deemed to occur on the later of: (i) the date the conduct ceased, or (ii) the date the Entity completed remediation; if remediation is not completed, the conduct is deemed continuous and the five-year period does not begin to run.

### 4.2.2 Public-Interest Discourse Carve-Out

Legitimate journalism, criticism, satire, commentary, scholarship, accountability reporting, and political speech directed at public figures concerning their public conduct, exercise of power, or matters of public concern do not, on their own, constitute harassment, incitement, or amplification for purposes of §4.2(d), (f), or (g).

This protection of public-interest discourse does not, however, extend to:

(i) calls for, organization of, or facilitation of physical violence;

(ii) doxxing, stalking, surveillance, or facilitation of stalking;

(iii) sexualized harassment, threats of sexual violence, or non-consensual intimate imagery;

(iv) campaigns directed at private individuals on the basis of their association with a public figure (such as family members, employees, friends, or correspondents);

(v) coordinated mass-harassment campaigns exceeding the bounds of public-interest critique; or

(vi) targeted harassment of journalists, dissidents, witnesses, or members of Protected or Vulnerable Groups for their identity, reporting, or advocacy.

The Contributor's Good-Faith Determination shall apply to whether particular conduct falls within the carve-out or its exceptions.

### 4.3 Safe-Harbor Exceptions

Research, journalism, archiving, documentation, accountability, whistleblowing, interoperability, or other harm-reduction efforts reasonably aimed at preventing, exposing, or mitigating the harms in §4 remain permitted for otherwise non-Prohibited Entities, provided that:

(a) the safe-harbor activity is the **dominant and bona fide purpose** of the Entity's use of the Work, not incidental to or in support of a Prohibited Use; and

(b) the Entity makes good-faith provision for transparency, publication, or public accountability appropriate to the activity.

The Contributor's Good-Faith Determination shall apply to whether the Safe-Harbor Exception is properly invoked.

(b) For §6.1(iv), reinstatement requires written consent from all affected Contributors.

Reinstatement under this subsection is a privilege, not a right, and the Contributor's Good-Faith Determination shall govern.
